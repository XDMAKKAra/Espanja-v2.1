## 1. Competitor Analysis

### WordDive (worddive.com)

Finnish edtech company, native Finnish product. Sells vocabulary learning for multiple languages including Spanish.

**What they do well:**
- Headline leads with outcome: "Opi 300 uutta sanaa kahdessa tunnissa" — concrete, testable claim.
- Trust signals are quantified: "yli 3 miljoonaa käyttäjää", "94% suositus".
- Social proof uses Finnish school context: "suosittu yli 200 koulussa".
- Pricing is prominently above the fold — they don't hide it.
- Mobile app prominent — App Store and Google Play CTAs in nav.
- Free trial is genuinely free (no card) and the freemium ceiling is generous enough to demonstrate value.

**What they don't do:**
- No exam-specific prep — WordDive is vocabulary, not YO-koe mechanics.
- No writing feedback — pure vocabulary drilling.
- No adaptive spaced repetition that maps to a specific test rubric.
- Finnish high school YO-koe is not their primary persona — they serve adults learning for travel and work too.

**Takeaway for Puheo:** WordDive wins on social proof volume (millions of users). Puheo can't compete there yet. Instead, lean hard on exam specificity — "built for the YO-koe rubric" is something WordDive cannot say. Also: put pricing earlier, quantify the free tier concretely.

### Mondly (mondly.com)

Global EdTech, gamified app. Has Spanish for Finnish speakers.

**What they do well:**
- Hero is pure outcome-framing: "Opi espanjaa nopeasti" with a live lesson demo embedded.
- Social proof: "110 miljoonaa oppijaa" — impossible to compete with on raw numbers.
- Streak mechanics and gamification visually prominent in hero screenshots.
- Pricing anchoring: monthly vs annual with % savings calculator.
- Multiple entry points: free web app, iOS, Android.

**What they don't do:**
- Zero YO-koe context — totally generic "learn Spanish" positioning.
- No writing assessment or rubric alignment.
- No adaptive level tracking against Finnish school criteria.
- Gamification optimizes for engagement, not exam performance.
- Finnish students actively know Mondly isn't exam prep — it's for hobby learning.

**Takeaway for Puheo:** Mondly is what Finnish students are already using when they're bored. Our message should address this: "Mondly on hyvä viihteeseen. Puheo on tehty YO-kokeeseen." Don't compete on fun — compete on results. The student's actual goal is an arvosana, not a streak.

---

### Duolingo (duolingo.com)

The default consumer language app. Every Finnish student knows it.

**What they do well:**
- Brand recognition means zero explanation needed in ads.
- Onboarding is exceptionally frictionless — start in 15 seconds without signup.
- Hero is interactive — you can start a lesson directly on the landing page.
- Free tier is genuinely useful.
- Push notifications and streak mechanics drive retention.

**What they don't do:**
- The YO-koe community openly agrees Duolingo doesn't prepare you for the exam.
- No grammar depth — Duolingo Spanish doesn't teach subjunctive conjugations seriously.
- No writing practice of any kind.
- No rubric alignment.
- Finnish-market localization is thin — no YTL content at all.

**Takeaway for Puheo:** Duolingo is the reference point in students' minds. They already know Duolingo is "not enough." Our hero message should validate this ("Olet jo kokeillut Duolingoa. Nyt tarvitset jotain muuta.") rather than ignoring it. This is a familiar pain point — use it.

---

### YLE Abitreenit (yle.fi/aihe/abitreenit)

Free, authoritative, produced by YLE in cooperation with YTL. The gold standard for past exam practice.

**What they do:**
- Complete archive of past YO-koe tasks for all subjects.
- Audio exercises from real exams.
- Zero cost, official authority.
- Trusted by every Finnish teacher.

**What they don't do:**
- Zero adaptive features — you get the exam as-is, no personalization.
- No AI feedback on writing — you compare to a model answer yourself.
- No organized study plan.
- No tracking of your performance over time.
- No explanation of errors.

**Takeaway for Puheo:** Abitreenit is the complement, not the enemy. The existing comparison table (index.html line 1123) is correct in tone. Reinforce: "Use Abitreenit for source material, use Puheo to understand why you're wrong."

## 2. Finnish EdTech Conventions

**Tone students expect:**
- Calm and direct — Finnish high schoolers are averse to American-style hype. "Nosta arvosanaasi 2 portaalla!" sounds fake.
- Respectful of intelligence — don't condescend. These are students taking a real exam, not toddlers learning ABCs.
- Honest about limitations — "tällä hetkellä vain lyhyt oppimäärä" builds trust. Overselling destroys it.
- Formal enough for parents, warm enough for students — a tone that works for both.
- No urgency tactics that feel manipulative. A countdown to the real exam date is fine (it's real). "Only 3 spots left!" is not.

**Trust signals Finnish students and parents respond to:**
- YTL/official curriculum alignment is the single highest-trust signal. Anything "YTL:n mukainen" immediately elevates credibility.
- Finnish origin ("Tehty Suomessa", "suomalainen tuote") matters more to parents than students.
- Teacher endorsement — even one named Finnish Spanish teacher saying "käytän tätä suosittelemiseen oppilailleni" would be gold.
- Privacy/GDPR compliance is expected as baseline, but stating it explicitly ("tietosi pysyvät EU:ssa") is reassuring.
- School logos or "käytetään X kouluissa" signals institutional validation.
- Specific numbers over vague superlatives: "500+ sanastotehtävää" > "satoja harjoituksia".

**What Finnish students expect from a learning site:**
- Clear free tier — if there's a paywall, it must be obvious and not feel tricky.
- Mobile-first — Finnish teenagers learn on phone during commute.
- Finnish language throughout — UI switching to English mid-flow is jarring and creates distrust.
- Fast load — Finnish broadband expectations are high. A slow initial load causes immediate bounce.
- Minimal account friction — Google SSO or email-only signup preferred.

**What Finnish parents expect:**
- Price in € with clear what-you-get breakdown.
- Refund policy clearly stated.
- Privacy policy accessible (GDPR).
- Contact info (email minimum, ideally Y-tunnus once registered).
- No auto-renewal surprises — one-time payment options preferred.

## 3. YO-koe SEO Keyword Research

### SEO alignment recommendations

1. **Index page** should rank for `espanja yo-koe 2026` and `lyhyt espanja yo-koe`.
2. **Blog** should target all grammar keywords — each post = one keyword.
3. **Diagnose page** should rank for `testaa espanjan taso` and `espanja taitotaso arvio`.
4. Current meta keywords (line 9) include `ojalá subjunktiivi` and `preteriti imperfekti` — these belong in blog posts, not the index page. Index page meta should focus on `espanja YO-koe 2026 harjoittelu`.
5. Blog posts lack internal linking to the app — every blog post should end with a relevant CTA linking to a matching exercise in-app.
