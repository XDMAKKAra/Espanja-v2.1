## Typography

**Headline — Crimson Pro 700 / Italic 600**
Rationale: Warmer and more approachable than Playfair Display, with a slightly handwritten quality that fits the "notebook" concept. Less magazine, more well-read reader's favourite. Excellent at display sizes and surprisingly good at body sizes too. Full Latin Extended. Free on Google Fonts.

**Body — Nunito 400/600/700**
Rationale: Rounded humanist sans — not childish, but friendlier than Source Sans. The slightly soft letterforms pair well with Crimson Pro's warmth without competing. Used widely in Finnish educational software. Full Latin Extended. Free on Google Fonts.

---

## Character

The only light mode of the three. Parchment backgrounds and ink-dark text feel like physical study materials — a reassurance signal for parents. The mustard accent reads as "quality" not "warning." Ruled paper texture on cards reinforces the studying metaphor without being literal.

**Best fit if:** Marcel wants the app to appeal directly to parents, or wants to differentiate from every other dark-mode study app. Also the most accessible (light-mode users with lower contrast sensitivity).

**Risk:** Light mode can feel less focused / more distracted than dark mode for actual study sessions. The brand might feel warm but also slightly less serious than the navy options. Mustard is the trickiest accent to execute consistently — it must stay golden-brown, not yellow, or it cheapens.
